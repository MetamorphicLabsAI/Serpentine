## ⚖️ Pricing Tier Analysis

The indie game market traditionally operates on three "impulse buy" thresholds.

### [A. THE IMPULSE ZONE ($4.99 - $5.99)]
- **Psychology:** "Pocket change" price. Extremely high conversion rate from screenshots alone.
- **Current Fit:** Serpentine fits this tier perfectly in its current state as a high-polish, lore-rich arcade game.
- **Revenue Model:** High volume, low friction. Ideal for mobile app stores and Itch.io.

### [B. THE PREMIUM INDIE ($9.99 - $12.99)]
- **Psychology:** "Subway Lunch" price. Players expect a "hook" and several hours of unique content.
- **Current Gap:** Requires 2-3 additional distinct game modes or a "Campaign" structure.
- **Revenue Model:** Higher per-unit profit. Suitable for a Steam launch with Steam Achievements/Trading Cards.

### [C. THE AA-INDIE ($14.99 - $19.99)]
- **Psychology:** "Pizza Night" price. Players expect meta-progression, boss fights, or multiplayer.
- **Current Gap:** Needs significant content breadth (20+ snakes, level editor, or local/online versus modes).
- **Revenue Model:** Maximizes fan value. Requires a strong "community mystery" marketing campaign.

3.  **Economy & Unlocks:** (ALREADY COMPLETED) - The Point Bank and Shop system encourages long-term retention. 

### [CONTENT EXPANSION ROADMAP]
- **SYSTEM BREACH:** A level-based "Puzzle" mode with static obstacles (Data Walls).
